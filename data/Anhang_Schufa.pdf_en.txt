2. Data processing by SCHUFA
2.1 Purposes of data processing and legitimate interests pursued by SCHUFA or a third party.
SCHUFA processes personal data in order to provide eligible recipients with information for assessing the creditworthiness of natural and legal persons. This includes calculating and transmitting score values. It only provides this information if a legitimate interest in doing so has been credibly demonstrated in each individual case and processing is permissible after weighing all interests. A legitimate interest exists in particular before entering into transactions with financial risk. The creditworthiness check serves to protect the recipients from losses in credit transactions and at the same time opens up the possibility of protecting borrowers from excessive debt through advice. The data is also processed for fraud prevention, assessment of trustworthiness, prevention of money laundering, identity and age verification, address verification, customer support or risk management, as well as for pricing or setting conditions. SCHUFA will inform about any changes to the purposes of data processing in accordance with Art. 14 para. 4 GDPR.
2.2 Legal basis for data processing.
SCHUFA processes personal data in accordance with the provisions of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The processing is based on consent and, where processing is necessary to protect the legitimate interests of the controller or a third party and does not outweigh the interests or fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject requiring the protection of personal data, on the basis of Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR. Consent can be revoked at any time from the relevant contractual partner. This also applies to consents that were given before the GDPR came into force. The revocation of consent does not affect the lawfulness of the personal data processed up to the time of revocation.
2.3 Origin of the data
SCHUFA receives its data from its contractual partners. These are institutions, financial companies, and payment service providers based in the European Economic Area and Switzerland, and possibly in other third countries (provided that an appropriate adequacy decision by the European Commission exists for these countries), which bear a financial default risk (e.g. banks, savings banks, cooperative banks, credit card, factoring and leasing companies), as well as other contractual partners who use SCHUFA products for the purposes mentioned in section 2.1, in particular from the (mail order) retail, e-commerce, service, rental, energy supply, telecommunications, insurance, or debt collection sectors. In addition, SCHUFA processes information from publicly accessible sources such as public directories and official announcements (debtor directories, insolvency announcements).
2.4 Categories of personal data that are processed (personal information, payment behavior, and contractual compliance).
• Personal data, e.g., name (including previous names, which may be provided upon separate request), first name, date of birth, place of birth, address, previous addresses
• Information about the opening and contractually compliant execution of an account (e.g., current accounts, installment loans, credit cards, accounts protected from seizure, basic accounts)
• Information about undisputed, overdue, and repeatedly demanded or legally enforced claims, as well as their settlement
• Information about abusive or other fraudulent behavior, such as identity or credit fraud
• Information from public registers and official announcements
• Score values
2.5 Categories of recipients of the personal data.
The recipients are contractual partners domiciled in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, and, where applicable, other third countries (provided that a corresponding adequacy decision by the European Commission exists for these countries), as per clause 2.3. Other recipients may include external contractors of SCHUFA as defined in Article 28 of the GDPR, as well as external and internal SCHUFA departments. SCHUFA is also subject to the statutory powers of intervention of state authorities.
2.6 Duration of data storage
SCHUFA stores information about individuals only for a specific period. The primary criterion for determining this period is necessity. To assess the necessity of continued storage or the deletion of personal data, SCHUFA has established standard deadlines. According to these deadlines, the standard storage period for personal data is three years from the date of its completion. Deviations from this rule include the following, which are deleted:

• Information about inquiries after twelve months.
• Information about trouble-free contract data for accounts that are documented without the associated claim (e.g., current accounts, credit cards, telecommunications accounts, or energy accounts), information about contracts for which the evidence check is legally required (e.g., accounts protected from seizure, basic accounts), and guarantees and commercial accounts that are creditor-managed, immediately after notification of termination.
• Data from the debtor registers of the central enforcement courts after three years, but earlier if SCHUFA can prove that the central enforcement court has ordered the deletion.
• Information about consumer/insolvency proceedings or debt relief proceedings exactly three years after the end of the insolvency proceedings or the granting of debt relief. In particularly complex individual cases, earlier deletion may also occur.
• Information about the rejection of an insolvency application due to insufficient assets, the cancellation of security measures, or the denial of debt relief, exactly three years later.
• Personal data relating to advance notices will be stored for exactly three years; after that, the necessity of continued storage will be assessed for a further three years. After that, they will be deleted, unless longer storage is necessary for identification purposes.

4. Profile creation (scoring)
The SCHUFA report can be supplemented with so-called score values. In scoring, a forecast is created for future events based on collected information and past experiences. The calculation of all score values at SCHUFA is generally based on the information stored at SCHUFA for the person in question, which is also disclosed in the report in accordance with Art. 15 of the GDPR. In addition, SCHUFA takes into account the provisions of § 31 BDSG when calculating the score. Based on the entries stored for a person, an assignment is made to statistical groups of people who have shown similar entries in the past. The method used is called "logistic regression" and is a well-founded, long-established, mathematical-statistical method for forecasting the probability of risk.
The following types of data are used by SCHUFA to calculate the score, although not every type of data is included in every individual score calculation: general data (e.g. date of birth, gender or number of addresses used in business transactions), previous payment defaults, credit activity in the last year, credit utilization, length of credit history, and address data (only if few personal, credit-relevant pieces of information are available). Certain information is neither stored nor taken into account when calculating score values, for example: information on nationality or special categories of personal data such as ethnic origin or information on political or religious beliefs in accordance with Art. 9 of the GDPR. The assertion of rights under the GDPR, such as access to the information stored at SCHUFA in accordance with Art. 15 of the GDPR, also has no influence on the score calculation.
The transmitted score values support the contractual partners in their decision-making and are incorporated into their risk management. The risk assessment and creditworthiness assessment are carried out solely by the direct business partner, as only this partner has access to numerous additional pieces of information – for example, from a credit application. This applies even if the partner relies solely on the information and score values provided by SCHUFA. A SCHUFA score alone is in any case not a sufficient reason to reject a contract.
Further information on creditworthiness scoring or the identification of unusual circumstances can be found at www.scoring-wissen.de.
